# Content Moderation (BRAM Brand Integrity)

Mastercard's Business Risk Assessment and Mitigation (BRAM) program includes specific
brand-integrity provisions that prohibit acquirers from processing transactions for
merchants engaged in activities that violate Mastercard's acceptable use standards.
Acme Pay conducts ongoing content monitoring of merchant storefronts and transaction
data to identify and remediate prohibited content.

## Prohibited categories

Merchants found to be selling or facilitating counterfeit goods, illegal drugs, adult
content that violates applicable law, goods or services that infringe intellectual
property rights, or gambling services in jurisdictions where such services are
restricted are subject to immediate review and, where confirmed, merchant suspension.

## Required actions

1. Review merchant storefront content on a weekly cadence using automated scanning
   combined with periodic manual sampling.
2. Flag any merchant whose content matches a prohibited category for human review
   within one business day.
3. Suspend the merchant's processing privileges pending investigation if prohibited
   content is confirmed.
4. Document all findings and remediation actions in the BRAM case file.

Source authority: Mastercard SPME §10.5.
