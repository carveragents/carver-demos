Department of Financial Protection and Innovation

DFPI Shuts Down Crypto Kiosk Operator for Cheating Consumers and Violating State Laws

View primary source

Published2026-05-18
Effective2026-05-20
Typeenforcement / regulatory_body
JurisdictionsUS-CA

What changed

DFPI ordered Anh Management LLC (Hermes Bitcoin) to cease all digital financial asset kiosk operations in California due to violations including excessive fees, transaction limits breaches, inadequate disclosures, and AML program failures.

Why it matters

This enforcement highlights regulatory scrutiny on crypto kiosk operators to prevent consumer fraud and ensure compliance with state and federal laws, reducing risks of scams and financial harm.

Key requirements

  • Cease all digital financial asset kiosk operations in California by May 20, 2026.
  • Comply with transaction limits (max $1,000 cash per day).
  • Charge fees within prescribed limits (max $5 or 15% of transaction value).
  • Provide clear pre-transaction disclosures in English and principal advertising language.
  • Issue receipts with required transaction details including fees and price spreads.
  • Implement effective Bank Secrecy Act/Anti-Money Laundering compliance programs including customer identification verification.

Penalties / consequences

  • Monetary penalties up to $5,000 per day or $2,500 per violation act under California Consumer Financial Protection Law.
  • Restitution payments to affected consumers.
  • Forced cessation of business operations in California.
  • Reputational damage impacting business viability.
