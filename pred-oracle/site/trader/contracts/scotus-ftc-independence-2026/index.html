Background: President Trump fired FTC Commissioners Rebecca Kelly Slaughter and Alvaro Bedoya in early 2025. The case challenges the 1935 Humphrey's Executor precedent, which prohibits presidents from removing commissioners of independent agencies without cause. A ruling for Trump would fundamentally restructure the FTC's independence from executive control and reshape antitrust enforcement authority.
