# Pred-Oracle Demo Video — Creative Brief

**Audience for this doc:** Media / video team building the demo asset.
**What you'll find here:** outcomes, constraints, priorities, what to skip. **Not a script and not a shot list** — narrative arc and pacing are yours.
**Source material:** the live static site (`site/`), three scenes: α Radar, γ Listing, β Expansion, plus a landing page.

**Status:** v2 — refreshed 2026-05-21 after two demo-video iterations against this brief.

## 1. The ask

Produce a **7–9 minute** demo video that we can send to senior leadership at a prediction-market platform (Kalshi, Polymarket, and the broader CFTC-licensed event-contract category) without a Carver person in the room. The video is the first touch — its job is to earn the second meeting.

## 2. Audience

Composite viewer is **General Counsel, Chief Compliance Officer, Head of Listing/Markets, and Head of International** at a prediction-market operator. They are:

- Sophisticated, time-poor, and skeptical of generic reg-intel pitches.
- Sitting at a regulatory intersection (CFTC + 50 state gambling commissions + foreign gambling regulators + named-entity contract resolution) that no incumbent vendor covers cleanly.
- Already burned by polished pitches that turn out to be vaporware.

Assume they will watch this once, possibly at 1.5×, possibly with the volume off for parts of it. Design for both.

## 3. The single commercial outcome

By the end of the video, the viewer should ask Carver for **one of two specific things**:

1. A paid pilot scoped to their platform's contracts and jurisdictions, or
2. A data/API exploration meeting with their engineering or markets team.

Anything softer ("interesting, let's talk again") = video did not land. Everything below is in service of producing one of these two asks.

## 4. What the viewer must believe at the end

Listed in priority order. The earlier ones are non-negotiable; the later ones are nice-to-haves we sacrifice if time gets tight.

1. **"This is tuned to OUR intersection, not generic reg-intel."** Carver knows the CFTC event-contract docket, state gambling commissions, foreign gambling regulators, and named-entity contract resolution. *Earned by:* landing-page stats + γ contract-specific timelines that name real regulators and real contracts.
2. **"This would have caught a real incident on our platform."** A concrete, named retrospective contract where Pred-Oracle's signal visibly leads up to the resolution event. *Earned by:* one γ retrospective contract, treated as the centerpiece.
3. **"My team's workflow fits into this tomorrow."** This isn't a research console — it's an inbox, a pre-listing gate, a heatmap. Familiar artifacts. *Earned by:* a glance at α's ticket inbox and β's heatmap, both treated as workflow surfaces, not analytics dashboards.
4. **"The depth is real, not staged."** Every visible value reconciles to a real corpus event. No lorem ipsum. No stock graphics. *Earned by:* simply being honest about what's on screen.
5. **"There is no incumbent doing this."** They mentally place Pred-Oracle in a new vendor box. *Earned by:* one explicit moment naming the gap in current vendor coverage.

## 5. Where to spend, where to skim

This is the most important section of the brief. **Don't try to do all three scenes justice in 7–9 minutes. You can't.**

| Scene | Treatment | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **γ — Listing** | **Centerpiece. Roughly half the runtime.** Centerpiece contract is locked to **`solana-etf-2025`** (20 events, richest signal, both Polymarket and Kalshi list spot-crypto-ETF contracts). Walk the timeline forward through time — camera scrolls **up** the page (newest events sit at top, oldest at bottom). Dwell on the per-event "why this matters" lines, show the conditions decomposition + storyline + heat panel. | This is the deepest, most differentiated, most defensible scene. It's where "wow" lives. |
| **α — Radar** | **Quick — operational vignette, not a tour.** Open the inbox, show one ticket flowing into an assignment, then a glance at the audit log. No need to walk every field. | Proves operational fit and "we'd use this Monday morning." Doesn't need depth to land. The audit log step is the proof point for "defensible to a regulator." |
| **β — Expansion** | **France-first calibrate-then-apply walk + a short cascade stop.** Heatmap opens on the France 13-month retrospective (calibration), then scrolls down to today's world heatmap + US states drilldown. Then a **~30s cascades stop** to surface the ESMA Q1 2026 risk note on event-contract platforms (this one slide names the audience's own business — keep it). Close with the quarterly report PDF artifact. | Proves the strategic layer exists. France is the canonical proof case; cascades is the live application of that pattern. |
| **Landing + close** | **30–45 seconds combined.** Don't recite the headline — let the page do that. Open with a smart one-liner that signals "three jobs, one data spine." Close on a CTA slate. | Frames the rest. |

**If a tradeoff is forced, cut from α or β before cutting from γ.**

## 6. Constraints

- **Live URLs only.** No staged screenshots, no Figma mockups, no animated overlays that don't exist on the actual page. The product must be the star.
- **Real data only.** Every contract, regulator, date, and event on screen is real. Do not fabricate examples.
- **No pricing.** Pricing is a follow-up conversation; the video proves uniqueness first.
- **No feature enumeration.** We don't list capabilities. We walk a narrative.
- **No "amazing" / "powerful" / "revolutionary" copy.** The product is the proof.
- **Voiceover allowed, on-screen text minimal.** If text is needed, it should match the site's typography (the site already uses a specific design system — please borrow from it rather than inventing new chrome).
- **Skip features that would need explanation.** If something requires more than one sentence to set up, cut it.
- **No persona names.** Personas are referred to by role only ("the General Counsel", "the Head of Listing", "the Head of International"). Site copy is already aligned; narration must match.
- **Brand naming is locked.** Intro/outro slides + brand badge use the **Carver Agents** wordmark (`_assets/carver_logo.png`). The product name **Pred-Oracle** appears as a subtitle. Don't substitute Pred-Oracle for the logo.
- **No "demo build" disclaimers.** Don't mention pre-caching, demo data, synthetic context, or anything that breaks the illusion that the viewer is watching the live product. The site footer already labels synthetic context where applicable; narration shouldn't reinforce it.
- **No full-headline recital in the opener.** The landing page headline is long ("...CFTC, 50 state gambling commissions, the SEC..."). The intro voiceover should NOT read it out — pick a sharper hook and let the page do the rest.
- **Polymarket-explicit on the France case.** When narrating β, name Polymarket and ANJ as the actual entities ("Polymarket was shut out of the French market by ANJ in December 2025…"). Don't soften to "a major prediction-market platform."

## 7. Anti-patterns to avoid

- ❌ Feature tour. We don't show every screen. Half the site stays unseen.
- ❌ Breadth flex ("49,735 events!") at the expense of depth. Depth on one contract beats counting events.
- ❌ Generic SaaS demo polish. The audience is allergic to it.
- ❌ Talking head over screen recording. The screen is the protagonist.
- ❌ Music that pulls focus. Atmosphere only, if at all.
- ❌ Closing with "contact sales." Close with a specific, lightweight next step (e.g. "send us one of your contract IDs and we'll show you what Pred-Oracle sees").

## 8. Observable success metrics

After the video ships, these are the signals we watch for:

1. Recipient asks for a follow-up meeting with **a specific scope** (their contracts, their jurisdictions), not a generic intro.
2. Recipient names a contract on their own platform and asks what Pred-Oracle would show for it.
3. Recipient forwards the video internally before the first reply (forwarding intent = the strongest signal).
4. Within five business days of send, Compliance or Listing-Risk reaches out to scope a pilot.

If we get (1) or (2) on more than 1 in 4 sends, the video is doing its job.

## 9. What to ignore from the rest of the repo

- Internal strategy docs (`docs/product-strategy.md`, `docs/LESSONS.md`) — context only, not source material for the video.
- The full `site/` map — many subpages exist but most are not needed. Stick to the scenes called out in §5.
- Build/codebase details — irrelevant to the video.

## 10. Decisions locked in v2 (read before pitching alternatives)

These were open in v1 and have been answered through iteration. Don't re-litigate without a strong reason.

- **γ centerpiece contract:** `solana-etf-2025` (20-event timeline, resolved end of 2025, listed by Polymarket).
- **β scope:** France-first calibrate-then-apply heatmap + ~30s cascades stop (ESMA event-contract risk note) + quarterly report. Cascades is in, not out.
- **Brand naming:** Carver Agents (logo + brand name on intro/outro/badge) + Pred-Oracle (product subtitle).
- **Persona names:** stripped — role labels only.
- **Runtime target:** ~7:30–8:00 final at 1.2× playback. Current take is **7:53**.
- **Narration mode:** voiceover (OpenAI cedar voice, ~140 wpm) with SRT sidecar captions.
- **Close:** outro slide with CTA ("Send us a contract ID — we'll show you what we see") + carveragents.ai URL.
- **France framing:** Polymarket-explicit, ANJ-explicit. AMF/ESMA/EU-Commission are the signal-trail proxies (ANJ itself not in Carver's corpus — call this out via the on-screen "Honest framing" callout, never claim ANJ events).
